<p><p>The fluorinated surfactants market is divided into two primary types: ionic and non-ionic. Ionic fluorinated surfactants are characterized by their charged functional groups, enabling strong interactions with various surfaces and enhancing stability in aqueous solutions. These are often used in industrial applications. In contrast, non-ionic fluorinated surfactants lack charge and are more versatile, providing improved emulsification and wetting properties. They are widely employed in cosmetics, cleaning products, and coatings, offering low toxicity and environmental impact.</p></p>
