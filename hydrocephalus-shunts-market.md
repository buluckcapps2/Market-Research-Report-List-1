<p><p>The hydrocephalus shunts market is divided into two primary types: adjustable valves and monopressure valves. Adjustable valves allow for post-operative modification of cerebrospinal fluid (CSF) flow, accommodating patient-specific needs and varying pressures. This flexibility enhances treatment outcomes. Monopressure valves, on the other hand, provide a fixed pressure level for CSF drainage, offering a simpler design with less potential for failure. Both types are crucial in managing hydrocephalus, ensuring optimal fluid balance and preventing complications.</p></p>
