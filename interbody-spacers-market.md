<p><p>The interbody spacers market comprises two primary types: Polyetheretherketone (PEEK) and Titanium. PEEK spacers are favored for their biocompatibility, flexibility, and radiolucency, allowing for better post-surgical imaging. Conversely, Titanium spacers offer superior strength and durability, promoting enhanced stability and load-bearing capabilities. The choice between these materials often depends on specific clinical requirements, patient needs, and surgeon preferences, impacting overall spinal fusion outcomes. Both materials play crucial roles in advancing spinal surgery technologies and improving patient care.</p></p>
