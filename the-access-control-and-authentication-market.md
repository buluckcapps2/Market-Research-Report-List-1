<p><p>The access control and authentication market is primarily categorized into Discretionary Access Control (DAC) and Mandatory Access Control (MAC). DAC allows resource owners to manage permissions, granting access based on individual discretion, which offers flexibility but can lead to security risks. MAC, on the other hand, enforces access policies established by a central authority, providing stricter controls suited for environments with sensitive information. Both models cater to varying security needs across industries, influencing their adoption and market growth.</p></p>
